What are organic products?


The definition of organic products is a product that is produced using organic farming methods, which limit modern synthetic inputs such as synthetic pesticides and chemical fertilizers. Organic products are also not processed using irradiation, industrial solvents, or chemical food additives.The organic agriculture movement emerged in the 1940s in response to agricultural industrialization which is now called the Green Revolution. Now various countries in the world implement food policies such as labeling organic certification so that a food can be sold to consumers as "organic food ingredients". With this regulation, organic food must be produced in a manner that is in accordance with organic standards applied by the government of a country and international organizations.

Advantages of organic products

1. Fewer pesticides

Pesticides are chemicals such as fungicides, herbicides and insecticides. These chemicals are widely used in conventional agriculture, and residues will remain attached to the food we eat. The rest of this pesticide cannot even be removed by the washing process, because it can be absorbed by the roots and deposited in the plants. These pesticide residues are neurotoxins that poison the nerves. Because it is free of chemicals, organic food crops are definitely healthier in the long run.

2. Fresher and last longer

Organic food is usually fresher and tastier when eaten. These naturally cultivated foods can also last longer, thus simultaneously minimizing the use of preservatives.

3. More nutritious

In addition to tastier taste, organic food according to research is also higher in the amount of nutrients. A study reported organic.org showed that organic fruits and vegetables contained 27% more vitamin C, 21.1% more iron, 29.3% more magnesium, 13.6% more phosphorus, and 18 % more polyphenols. Researchers have found that salicylic acid averages 600% in organic soup, and organic carrot soup contains 1,040 nanograms of salicylate, compared to non-organic carrot soup which is only 20 nanograms. Organic meat also contains a little saturated fat, and more is healthy fats.

4. Does not contain antibiotics

Organic animals are not given antibiotics, growth hormones, or artificial foods from side ingredients / waste. The use of antibiotics in the production of broiler livestock will create new strains of bacteria that are resistant to antibiotics. This means that when a person falls ill because of this strain, he will respond less to antibiotic treatment.

5. Not genetically engineered

With the advancement of technology, genetic engineering for food has now become commonplace to increase production. The study said that mice in the laboratory that were given genetically modified corn developed tumors in brain and eventually died. Organic food is the only food that is free from crosses.

6. Organic farming is more environmentally friendly

Organic farming has been shown to reduce pollution (air, water and soil), save water, reduce erosion, increase soil fertility, and use less energy. In addition, organic farming is better for ecosystems that are nearby. Agriculture without pesticides is also safer for farmers who manage food production.
